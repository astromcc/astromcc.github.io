A Thermal Spectrum

  • Objects emit light, even if that light is not visible light. The amount of light emitted at each wavelength depends on the absolute temperature of the object, measured in Kelvins.
  • A thermal spectrum is a special kind of light spectrum that depends on the object's temperature only. Another term for this kind of spectrum is a blackbody spectrum. The ideal object emitting the spectrum is described as a blackbody.
