A Demonstration of Bayes' Theorem

  • Thomas Bayes (1701 - 1761) was an English mathematician, philosopher, and Presbyterian minister who developed a new way of thinking about data and probability.
  • His central concept: We update our knowledge as data are collected.
  • These data may be observations or measurements of the natural world or data collected during an experiment.
  • This box below demonstrates a simple example of Bayes' theorem. Your task is use the available data to predict where a dot is hidden somewhere within the white box.
  • This process is a simple model of how scientists gain confidence in their knowledge of the natural world.
  • Initially the white box below is empty. You have no information about the position of the hidden dot.
  • You can make an initial guess about the location of the dot by clicking within the white box.
  • Make a measurement by clicking the Measure button. A black dot will appear to mark the data point.
  • A colored region shows if the hidden dot is left, right, above, or below the data point.
  • After you add a data point, update your prediction by clicking in the white box again.
  • Add more data points and continue to update your prediction.
  • If you feel confident in the hidden dot's location, click the Reveal button to display the hidden dot.
  • You can start a new search by clicking the Restart button.
