A Demonstration of Bayes' Theorem

  • Thomas Bayes (1701 - 1761) was an English mathematician, philosopher, and Presbyterian minister who developed a new way of thinking about data and probability.
  • His central concept: We update our knowledge as data are collected.
  • These data may be observations or measurements of the natural world or data collected during an experiment.
  • The white box below is a simple playing field to illustrate Bayes' theorem. Your task is use the available data to identify where a hidden triangle is located somewhere within the white box.
  • This process is a simple model of how scientists gain confidence in their knowledge of the natural world.
  • Initially the white box below is empty. You have no information about the position of the hidden triangle.
  • Click within the white box to make a guess about the location of the hidden triangle. It is marked by an orange dot.
  • Click the Measure button to make a measurement of the hidden triangle. A black dot marks a random spot within the white box.
  • A colored region appears as well. The colored region shows whether the hidden triangle is to the left, right, above, or below the black dot.
  • Click inside the white box again to update your knowledge of the hidden triangle.
  • You may click the Measure button and click inside the white box as many times as you wish.
  • Click the Reveal button to unveil the triangle's true location.
  • Click the Restart button to begin a new search for the triangle at a new location.
