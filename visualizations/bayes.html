A Demonstration of Bayes' Theorem

  • Thomas Bayes (1701 - 1761) was an English mathematician, philosopher, and Presbyterian minister who developed a new way of thinking about data and probability.
  • His central concept: We update our knowledge as data are collected.
  • Data may be observations or measurements of the natural world or may be collected during an experiment.
  • The white box below is a simple region in which to illustrate Bayes' theorem. Your task is to use data to locate a small triangle is hidden somewhere within the white box.
  • This process is a simple model of how we gain confidence in our knowledge of the natural world.
  • Initially the white box is empty. You have no information about the position of the hidden triangle.
  • Click inside the white box to place an orange dot, marking your guess about the location of the hidden triangle.
  • Click the Measure button below to make a measurement of the hidden triangle. As a result...
    • a black dot and a colored region appear in the white box,
    • the black dot marks a random measurement within the white box,
    • the colored region marks where the triangle is relative to the dot.
  • Clicking inside the white box after a measurement updates your idea of the hidden triangle's location.
  • Click the Measure button as many times as you wish and update your idea of the triangle's location, based on the measurements, as many times as you wish.
  • Click the Reveal button to display the triangle's true location.
  • Click the Restart button to begin a new search for the triangle at a new location.
